The Historic 2023 NL MVP Race in the MLB

By Gavin Murray

After a season filled with many surprises, the 2023 Major League Baseball Campaign has officially come to a close. Thrilling comebacks, memorable postseason games, and a lop-sided World Series highlighted the year. One of the biggest stories, however, came from five of the games’ very best players: Ronald Acuna JrMookie Betts, Freddie Freeman, Matt Olson, and Corbin Carroll. This elite group battled against one another to give fans the most competitive MVP race baseball fans have seen in quite some time. This article will be a statistical analysis of the performances that each of these players put on to see if Ronald Acuna Jr. was deserving of the award, or if someone else might have been robbed. Comparing the Candidates

A closer look at the 5 players who all had a shot

Olson put up monster numbers this past year, slugging 54 homeruns, leading all of MLB. (Photo credits to Alex Lord)

This year’s sprint to the finish line featured several standout names. Ronald Acuna Jr. was a feel good story for any sports fan. 2 years ago, Acuna suffered a season-ending ACL tear in Miami, which derailed what could’ve been an anonymous MVP in his catalog. The following year, he struggled at the plate, with many experts and fans questioning if he’d ever be the same player again. Acuna silenced the doubters, going on to have 41 homers, 217 total hits, and 73 stolen bases, an MLB record. He also helped lead the Braves to the best record in all of baseball.

Acuna’s teammate, Matt Olson, also chipped in during a historic season in Atlanta. The power-hitting first baseman clobbered 54 home runs and drove in 139 runs, leading all of baseball in both categories. Olson was also awarded his first career silver slugger, an honor given out to the best offensive player at each position.

The 1998 NL MVP race in comparison to this year

2023 reached the same competiveness of the "Steriod Era"

Sosa (left) and McGwire had one of the most memorable MVP races of all time in any sport. (Photo credits to ESPN)

It’s been a while since the league has seen an MVP race as competitive as this one. In years past, there has typically been a run-away candidate who blows past the rest of the competition. This year was different, however, and according to statistics expert Megan Carroll, this race rivaled one that took place 25 years ago.

“When looking at the data, I couldn’t help but think back to the ‘Steroid Era’ of baseball,” said Megan, who was a big Yankees fan growing up. “It brought more attention to a sport that many believe was on the downfall."

Megan brings up a very interesting point. Baseball has been regarded as a “dying sport” for the last few decades, with many believing that it will never reach the level of popularity that the MLB and NFL are at. The “Steroid Era,” which ran from 1994 to 2004, featured several players who were casually hitting 60 to 70 home runs in a season, something that seems impossible to do now.

The 1998 season featured one of the most memorable MVP races in any sport, ever. Mark McGwire and Sammy Sosa each put together jaw-dropping seasons and also battled for the MVP and the home run title for 1998. What made it even more entertaining was that McGwire and Sosa both played in the same division, with McGwire playing for the St. Louis Cardinals and Sosa playing for the Chicago Cubs.

Did the voters get it right?

Was Ronald Acuna Jr. deserving of MVP, or was someone robbed?

Corbin Carroll led the D-Backs to the World Series while winning NL Rookie of the Year. (Photo credits to Mark J. Rebilas)

A few days after the season concluded, it would officially be announced that Ronald Acuna Jr. won the National League Most Valuable Player award for the 2023 season. After the announcement, several baseball fans took to the internet to voice out their opinions, with many claiming that someone else should have won the award. Sure, Acuna might not have led the league in homeruns or batting average, but according to the stats, he added the most value to the team. His production offensively and defensively was able to win the voters over in the end.
